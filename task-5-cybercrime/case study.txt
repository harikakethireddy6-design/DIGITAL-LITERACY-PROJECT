Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to trick people into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, by pretending to be a trusted source. 
This usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that look very similar to official ones.
For example, a student may receive an email that appears to be from a bank or a popular website asking them to verify their account.
The email contains a link that leads to a fake website.
When the student enters their login details, the attacker collects this information and gains unauthorized access to their account.
Phishing attacks often target students and general internet users because they may not always verify the authenticity of messages.
The consequences include loss of personal data, financial loss, and misuse of accounts.
This type of cybercrime highlights the importance of being cautious while clicking links and sharing personal information online.
